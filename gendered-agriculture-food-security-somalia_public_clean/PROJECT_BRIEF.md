# Project brief

## Title

**Beyond Household Headship: Gendered Agriculture and Food-Security Pathways in Somalia**

## Purpose

This mini-project turns a survey-based research idea into a clean public package. It shows how gender-sensitive food-security analysis can be strengthened by looking beyond household-head sex and paying attention to agricultural roles.

## Why it matters

Household-head sex is often used as a quick gender marker in food-security analysis. However, agricultural livelihoods are role-based. Different people may operate land, contribute labour, manage crops, participate in livestock byproduct decisions, sell produce, hire labour, and respond to shocks.

A role-sensitive approach can help improve agricultural extension, food-security targeting, climate advisory services, social protection design and resilience programming.

## Public outputs

- Aggregate indicator tables
- Conceptual pathway figure
- Crop-category visual
- Adjusted-association visual
- Public dashboard card
- Data privacy note
- Reproducible figure script
- LinkedIn/Facebook launch post

## Responsible data approach

The project shares only derived aggregate outputs. Raw household microdata are not included.
